How the router decides where a document goes

Layer 1 — name and note free · instant · nothing leaves Google

Deterministic keyword rules run on the filename and your one-line note. "ATB Invoice March.pdf" routes to Finance / Invoices with no reading required. This handles the clear majority of everyday documents at zero cost, and it is the layer running live on this page.

Layer 2 — read the contents OCR · stays inside Google

When the name says nothing ("IMG_2039.pdf"), the text is read. Google Drive OCRs uploads automatically and Google Document AI extracts vendor, date, amount, and document type off a receipt, invoice, or contract, all inside your own Google Cloud. The extracted text is fed back into the Layer 1 rules.

Layer 3 — judgment on the ambiguous ones a model, in-boundary for sensitive

When rules plus extracted text are still not confident, the text goes to a model that classifies it into the taxonomy. Sovereign and confidential material (legal, cap table, incorporation) runs on Gemini via Google Vertex, inside Google's trust boundary. The model only ever suggests; a human approves anything sovereign or confidential. Anything still unsure routes to the drive's _INBOX for the weekly human sweep.

Layers 2 and 3 run server-side in your Google Apps Script Web App (02_RoutingRules.gs). Paste that endpoint into the config at the top of this page's script and the "File it to Drive" button sends the document through the full pipeline. Until then, this page previews the destination and links you into Drive to file it yourself.
